# Naming — working palette

Brief: name the **instrument**, and ideally a **naming system** for engines/modes and
presets. Pascal names deliberately (story > cleverness). Strands he wants in the mix:
aviation, literature/sci-fi, music/synth history, Dutch/European, and **geek/hacker /
chiptune / demoscene** ("8-bit music inspired set").

## Proposed naming *system* (the strongest idea)
- **Instrument** → aviation / Dutch (Pascal flies; the badge is Dutch). A name with weight.
- **Engines / voice models** → legendary **sound chips & sizecoding synths** (the hacker
  strand): a coherent set as we add `SynthModel`s.
- **Presets / banks** → playful demoscene / chiptune-era references.

This gives a story: *an aviation-named instrument that hosts chip-named engines.*

## Instrument candidates
1. **Fokker** — Anthony Fokker, Dutch aviation pioneer. Aviation **and** Dutch in one word;
   fat, punchy, a touch cheeky to an English ear. Resonates with the badge's Dutch origin.
2. **Constellation** (call-sign **"Connie"**) — Lockheed's elegant triple-tail airliner;
   "constellation" = stars = the *sparkling highs* we're chasing. Long but gorgeous.
3. **Pander** — Pander & Son, obscure Dutch aircraft maker (the S.4 Postjager). Short,
   unusual, ownable; deep-cut Dutch aviation.
4. **Klang** — German "sound/timbre"; nods to **4klang**, the famous 4-kilobyte demoscene
   softsynth — perfect for a memory-limited synth. Clean, meaningful, European + hacker.
5. **Rausch** — after **Farbrausch**, demoscene legends of fitting whole synths in 64 kB;
   "Rausch" = rush/intoxication/noise. Pure hacker-music cred; on-theme for our constraints.
6. **Hex** — Discworld's thinking machine (Pratchett). Short, techy, badge-friendly; the
   literary/geek wink. (Check collision with other audio "Hex" products.)
7. **Hooloovoo** — Hitchhiker's "hyperintelligent shade of the colour blue." Niche, fun,
   very Pascal; a mouthful.

## Engine / mode names (sound-chip & sizecoder homage)
- **SID** (C64 MOS 6581/8580 — patron saint of chiptune), **POKEY** (Atari), **AY** /
  YM2149 (ZX/Amstrad/MSX), **2A03** (NES), **OPL** / YM3812 (AdLib — *FM*, fits our FM mode),
  **SCC** (Konami), **4klang** / **V2** (Farbrausch softsynth). e.g. the VA engine could be
  the analog one and the FM engine nods to **OPL**; a chip-accurate mode could be **SID**.

## Recommendation to riff on
- Instrument: **Fokker** (aviation+Dutch, fat, memorable) or **Klang** (hacker + literally
  "timbre", ties to 4klang). My lean: **Fokker** for the instrument, **Klang** as a
  fallback / sub-brand for the engine framework.
- Engines named for chips (**OPL** for FM, **SID** for a lo-fi/chip mode, etc.).

Not precious — these are anchors to react to. Pascal collaborates on names (the Monarch
precedent). Pick, mix, or reject freely.
